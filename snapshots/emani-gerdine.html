Overview

Emani brings a professional healthcare perspective to the AI debate, arguing that students should learn to use AI responsibly rather than be forbidden from using it. Her "third space" concept—AI as cognitive partner rather than cheating tool or struggle-replacement—has sparked productive responses from classmates. She introduces the concept of "ethical opacity," arguing that creators should be judged on output quality, not process surveillance.
