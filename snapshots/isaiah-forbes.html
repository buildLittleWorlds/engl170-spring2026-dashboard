Overview

Isaiah explores technology as a tool that amplifies human capability in domains where precision and pattern recognition matter most -- criminal investigations and creative work. His two posts cover the extremes of AI's application: one examines how machine learning and data mining help solve cold cases and identify suspects; the other argues that digital writing tools unlock creative potential that handwriting alone constrains. The connecting thread is that AI and digital technology are not replacements for human judgment but instruments that expand what humans can do and find.

Key Themes

  • AI in criminal investigation
  • Digital tools and creative potential
  • Technology as amplifier of human capability
  • Pattern recognition and data mining

Posts

Beyond the Ink: Why the Digital Pen is the Key to Unlocking Creative Potential February 2, 2026

Argues that digital writing and creative tools -- styluses, tablets, AI-assisted drafting -- do not diminish creative work but expand access to it. The "ink" metaphor frames traditional writing as a constraint; the "digital pen" removes friction between thought and expression, enabling writers and artists to iterate faster and reach further than analog tools allow.

The Digital Detective: How AI is Finding Out About Murders January 21, 2026

Examines AI's role in criminal investigations -- facial recognition, predictive policing, digital forensics, and data mining across communication records. Argues that AI functions as a "digital detective" that processes evidence at a scale no human investigator could manage, helping law enforcement identify suspects and solve cold cases. Raises implicit questions about accuracy, bias, and civil liberties that frame the technology as powerful but consequential.
