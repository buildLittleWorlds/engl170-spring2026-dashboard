Overview

Tom examines AI's practical applications in sports, focusing on boxing. His approach emphasizes AI as a supportive tool that enhances human performance without replacing the core human elements that make sports meaningful. Central insight: AI can make training smarter, fights safer, and judging fairer, but "courage, discipline, creativity, and heart cannot be programmed into an algorithm."

Key Themes

  • Performance analysis
  • Injury prevention
  • AI-assisted judging
  • Human-AI balance

Posts

How Artificial Intelligence Is Improving Boxing January 23, 2026

Comprehensive survey of AI applications in boxing: training analysis (tracking hand speed, punch accuracy, reaction time), opponent analysis (scanning fight footage for habits and tendencies), injury prevention (wearables monitoring impact forces and concussion risk), recovery optimization (ML algorithms for sleep/nutrition/training schedules), AI-assisted judging (tracking clean punches for fairer outcomes), and enhanced broadcasting (real-time statistics for fans). Concludes that AI serves as a "powerful tool that supports human decision-making" while the human elements of courage and creativity remain irreplaceable.
