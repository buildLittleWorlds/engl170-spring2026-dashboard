Overview

Eliana tackles the AI conversation with rigorous research and comparative analysis, drawing on academic studies, policy analysis, and close engagement with primary sources like Ted Chiang's Princeton lecture and Steve Yegge's "vibe coding" concept. Her writing systematically dismantles viral claims about AI's environmental impact while engaging seriously with educational critiques. Central thesis: thinking has relocated rather than disappeared—from sentence-level execution to higher-order judgment, evaluation, and architectural oversight. She also brings a creative arts perspective, defending art as a "social contract between two conscious minds."

Core Arguments

The Environmental Data

AI environmental claims are "mathematically illiterate" when compared to legacy industries. The textile industry accounts for 20% of global wastewater; traditional banking consumes ~263 TWh/year; gold mining uses 131 TWh. A single ChatGPT query uses ~2 milliliters of water; one pair of jeans requires 7,500 liters. AI data centers are increasingly powered by renewables and can be tools for environmental remediation.

The Cognitive Relocation Thesis

Engaging Ted Chiang vs. Steve Yegge: Chiang locates thinking at the sentence level; Yegge describes "vibe coding" where experts orchestrate systems through natural language. Eliana argues thinking has relocated—from "How do I implement this function?" to "Is this the right approach?" The expertise Chiang values still exists, but expressed through "the right questions" rather than "the right semicolons."

The Human Spark

Human creativity is not merely a process of output—it is an act of translation. Artists translate a complex web of sensory experiences, trauma, joy, and cultural nuances into a medium. AI cannot replicate this because AI does not "experience" the world. Large Language Models function through stochastic parity—they are "mirrors, not windows." But AI can serve as a "brainstorming partner," helping overcome the blank page.

Art as Social Contract

Art's true worth lies in incommensurable qualities: challenging perception, fostering empathy, recording the feeling of history. AI-generated art is "hollow"—a statistical arrangement lacking emotional intentionality. Because there is no "self" behind the machine, there is no true communication. Art is a "social contract between two conscious minds"; replacing artists with AI is the ultimate "hyper-commodification."
