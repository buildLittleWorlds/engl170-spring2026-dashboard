Response to: The Ethical Limit of Injury Prediction (Sam Levine)
Sam Levine recently raised a flag regarding the "ethical limits" of AI injury prediction. In The Optimization Protocol, we have a different word for "the spirit of the game": unmanaged risk.
The Physics of the Break
An injury is the mathematical conclusion of accumulated mechanical stress. Research in The British Journal of Sports Medicine highlights that "workload monitoring" via AI can significantly reduce non-contact injuries by identifying physiological red zones before failure occurs. If an AI model identifies a 90% probability of a tear, the "ethical limit" isn't about whether we should intervene—it's that we are morally obligated to.
From Prediction to Restoration
The protocol should initiate a targeted biochemical intervention. Regenerative peptides like BPC-157 aren't "doping" agents; they are biological project managers that accelerate angiogenesis and fibroblast proliferation. We should view the athlete’s body like a high-performance jet engine: we don't wait for the engine to explode to prove the pilot is "natural"; we use predictive maintenance to ensure it never happens.
